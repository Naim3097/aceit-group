The Dark Side of Smart Tech

Picture this. You get an email that looks exactly like it is from your CEO, asking for a fund transfer. The tone is right. The signature is perfect. It even references a real project.

That is AI-powered phishing.

Or imagine watching a deepfake video of a company leader saying something they never said. That is not a sci-fi trailer. That is the current reality of AI-generated cyber threats.

And malware? It has evolved. It now learns how you work, adapts to your systems, and becomes harder to trace every time it reappears.

It is fast. It is quiet. And it is relentless.

Simple Things You Can Do Today

This is not just a tech team problem. Every person in the business is part of the defence.

Here is what you can start doing now:

Stop clicking on suspicious links. Even if they look familiar.

Update your software. Yes, it is boring. But yes, it matters.

Report anything that feels off. It is better to be cautious than compromised.

And most importantly, do not try to do it all on your own.
