1.0 Information Security Policy Statement

AceTeam Networks Sdn Bhd is committed to ensuring the continuity of its business via the implementation of an international standard for information security: ISO 27001:2022 Information Security Management System (ISMS). This Policy shall describe the way AceTeam business operates, internal and external factors influencing it and highlight the potential consequences of a security breach.

Policy Objectives

  • Ensure compliance with legal requirements, regulations, and guidelines
  • Provide guidelines for protecting valuable information resources from theft, damage, denial of service and unauthorized access or change of information
  • Increase user awareness of their responsibilities when using AceTeam resources
  • Ensure that AceTeam is capable of continuing their services when any related security incident occurs
  • Ensure the protection of the organization and personal data privacy
  • Ensure the availability and reliability of the system integration services supplied and operated by AceTeam
  • Ensure that external service providers comply with AceTeam's information security needs and requirements
  • Improve and strengthen the implementation of an internationally recognized ISMS

Core Security Principles

Confidentiality: The property that information is made available or disclosed to only authorized individuals, entities, or processes.

Integrity: The property of safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of assets where information can be modified only by authorized individuals, entities, or processes.

Availability: The property of being accessible and usable upon demand by an authorized individuals, entities, or processes.

This Policy is applied to the entire Information Security Management System (ISMS), as defined in the ISMS Scope Document. Users of this document are all employees of AceTeam Networks Sdn Bhd, as well as relevant external parties.

3.0 Managing Information Security

3.1 Information Security Strategy

AceTeam's current business strategy and framework for risk management are the guidelines for identifying, assessing, evaluating, and controlling information related risks through establishing and maintaining the Information Security Policy.

Security Framework Components

  • Risk assessment and treatment processes aligned with ISO 27001:2022
  • Security policy development and implementation across all departments
  • Asset classification and handling procedures for sensitive information
  • Access control and identity management systems
  • Incident response and business continuity planning
  • Regular security audits and compliance reviews

Technical Security Measures

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols
  • Multi-factor authentication for system access and privileged accounts
  • Network segmentation and firewall protection
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS)
  • Regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
  • Secure backup and disaster recovery systems with regular testing

Physical Security Controls

  • Controlled access to facilities and server rooms with badge systems
  • 24/7 surveillance and monitoring systems at all locations
  • Environmental controls including fire suppression and climate control
  • Secure disposal of physical media and equipment following data sanitization procedures

4.0 Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) Compliance

4.1 PDPA Overview and Commitment

AceTeam Networks Sdn Bhd is committed to full compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA) of Malaysia. We recognize the importance of protecting personal data and ensuring that all data processing activities are conducted in accordance with Malaysian law and international best practices.

This section outlines our comprehensive approach to personal data protection, including data collection, processing, storage, and disposal procedures that align with PDPA requirements and our commitment to maintaining the highest standards of data protection.

4.2 Personal Data Definition and Scope

Under PDPA, personal data refers to any information that can identify a living individual, either directly or indirectly. At AceTeam, we handle various types of personal data including:

  • Employee Data: Names, IC numbers, addresses, contact details, employment records, payroll information, and performance data
  • Client Data: Business contact information, technical specifications, project details, and authorized personnel records
  • Vendor Data: Supplier contact information, contract details, and business relationship records
  • Sensitive Data: Any data requiring special protection under PDPA including financial information and confidential business data

4.3 PDPA Principles Implementation

AceTeam implements all seven PDPA principles to ensure comprehensive data protection:

PDPA Principle Implementation at AceTeam Responsibility
General Principle Personal data processed lawfully and fairly with appropriate security measures Data Protection Officer
Notice & Choice Clear notification of data collection purposes with opt-in/opt-out mechanisms HR Department
Disclosure Personal data disclosed only for stated purposes and to authorized parties CISO
Security Appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data IT Security Team
Retention Personal data retained only as long as necessary for stated purposes Data Protection Officer
Data Integrity Personal data kept accurate, complete, and up-to-date Department Heads
Access Data subjects can access and correct their personal data upon request HR Department

4.4 Data Subject Rights

AceTeam respects and facilitates the exercise of data subject rights under PDPA. Individuals have the right to request information about their personal data and how it is processed.

Your Rights Include:

  • Right to be informed about data collection and processing purposes
  • Right to access personal data held by AceTeam
  • Right to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to limit processing of personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to withdraw consent for data processing where applicable
  • Right to request data portability where technically feasible
  • Right to lodge complaints with relevant authorities

To exercise these rights, data subjects may contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@aceteam.com.my or submit a written request to our registered office address.

4.5 Data Security Measures

AceTeam implements comprehensive technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction:

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest
  • Role-based access controls with least privilege principles
  • Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Regular security updates and patch management
  • Network monitoring and threat intelligence systems

Physical Security

  • Secure facilities with controlled access points
  • 24/7 surveillance systems at all locations
  • Environmental controls to protect equipment
  • Secure storage and disposal of physical documents

Administrative Controls

  • Security policies and procedures documented and enforced
  • Mandatory staff training on data protection and security
  • Background checks for employees handling personal data
  • Regular security assessments and audits

Incident Response

  • Data breach detection and response procedures
  • Notification protocols for authorities and affected individuals
  • Remediation processes to prevent recurrence
  • Documentation and reporting of security incidents

4.6 Data Retention and Disposal

AceTeam maintains a data retention schedule that ensures personal data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected or as required by law:

Data Category Retention Period Disposal Method
Employee Records 7 years after employment termination Secure deletion/shredding
Client Information Duration of contract + 3 years Secure deletion/shredding
Financial Records 7 years as per legal requirements Secure deletion/shredding
Security Logs 2 years minimum Automated secure deletion

All disposal methods comply with industry best practices and ensure complete data destruction to prevent unauthorized recovery or reconstruction of personal data.

4.7 Third Party Data Sharing

AceTeam may share personal data with third parties only under specific circumstances and with appropriate safeguards in place:

Permitted Data Sharing Scenarios

  • With explicit consent from the data subject
  • For legitimate business purposes as notified to data subjects
  • To comply with legal obligations or court orders
  • With service providers under strict contractual obligations
  • For emergency situations involving health or safety

Data Processing Agreements: All third-party data processors are required to sign Data Processing Agreements (DPA) that ensure compliance with PDPA requirements and maintain the same level of data protection as AceTeam.

Third-Party Vetting Process

  • Security assessment of all third-party vendors
  • Contractual obligations for data protection compliance
  • Regular audits of third-party data handling practices
  • Right to audit and inspect third-party operations

4.8 Data Breach Management

AceTeam has established comprehensive data breach response procedures to ensure prompt detection, assessment, and response to any personal data security incidents:

Detection & Assessment

  • 24/7 monitoring systems for security incidents
  • Immediate incident classification procedures
  • Impact assessment to determine severity and scope
  • Documentation of all breach details and timeline

Containment & Recovery

  • Immediate containment measures to stop data exposure
  • System isolation to prevent further compromise
  • Data recovery procedures from secure backups
  • Forensic analysis to identify root cause

Notification Procedures

  • Relevant authorities notified within 72 hours of breach detection
  • Affected individuals informed without undue delay
  • Clear communication about the nature and extent of the breach
  • Information provided on steps taken to mitigate harm

Post-Incident Actions

  • Comprehensive incident documentation and reporting
  • Post-incident review to identify lessons learned
  • Implementation of corrective actions to prevent recurrence
  • Updates to policies and procedures based on findings
